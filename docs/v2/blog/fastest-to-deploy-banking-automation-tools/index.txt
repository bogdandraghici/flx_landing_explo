<h2 id="why-do-most-banking-automation-projects-miss-compliance-deadlines">Why do most banking automation projects miss compliance deadlines?</h2>

<h3 id="which-interpretation-are-you-actually-deploying">Which interpretation are you actually deploying?</h3>

<li><strong>Screen-scraping RPA</strong>: fragile against any core UI change; rarely survives a model-risk review.</li>
<li><strong>Low-code BPM</strong>: long integration cycles into a legacy core (such as an older mainframe or COBOL-era system) typically consume the runway before testing begins.</li>
<li><strong>Agentic AI on general-purpose LLM platforms</strong>: non-deterministic outputs that fail the audit trails required under regulator-grade controls.</li>

<h2 id="how-should-a-bank-sequence-a-6-month-automation-rollout-to-hit-the-deadline">How should a bank sequence a 6-month automation rollout to hit the deadline?</h2>
<p>A bank should sequence a six-month automation rollout in tightly scoped phases, treating each month as a milestone gate rather than a status check — and aligning the sequence to the decision-stage of the journey, where the CDO, CRO, and Head of Operations need evidence of audit-readiness before they will green-light scale.</p>
<p>The rollout below assumes a Tier 1 or Tier 2 bank with a hard regulatory deadline (for example, a DORA-adjacent obligation, an AML remediation order, or a 2026 supervisory commitment) and an existing legacy core.</p>
<h3 id="what-is-the-month-by-month-sequence">What is the month-by-month sequence?</h3>
<ol>
<li><strong>Month 1 — Scope and signal lock-in.</strong> Select one workflow with measurable regulatory exposure (commercial onboarding, KYC refresh, or claims triage). Lock the success metric — typically time-to-yes, false-positive rate, or handoff count — and freeze the audit-trail requirements with the Model Risk Officer.</li>
<li><strong>Month 2 — Integration scaffolding.</strong> Stand up the platform inside your own VPC on AWS, Azure, or GCP, or on-premise, so regulated data never leaves your perimeter. Establish read-only access to your system of record (core banking, CRM, document store) before any agent writes back.</li>
<li><strong>Month 3 — Pre-built agent assembly.</strong> Configure pre-built banking agents — for example, an AML false-positive screener or a commercial onboarding orchestrator — rather than custom-building from zero. This is where weeks-not-months timelines are typically won or lost.</li>
<li><strong>Month 4 — Shadow-mode pilot.</strong> Run agents in parallel to human operators on live traffic. Capture deterministic decision logs for every step so model-risk review has a complete evidence pack.</li>
<li><strong>Month 5 — Controlled production cutover.</strong> Move one segment (a branch, a product line, or a customer tier) into agent-led processing. Keep a human-in-the-loop gate on exception paths.</li>
<li><strong>Month 6 — Scale and regulator pack.</strong> Expand to full volume, freeze the audit bundle, and deliver the supervisory submission.</li>
</ol>
<h3 id="which-milestones-are-non-negotiable">Which milestones are non-negotiable?</h3>
<p>The decision-stage gates that cannot slip are: signed-off audit-trail design (end of month 2), shadow-mode evidence pack (end of month 4), and a deterministic-output certification before production cutover. Miss any of these, and the deadline becomes a compliance event rather than a delivery event.</p>
